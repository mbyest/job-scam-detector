üìã Project Overview

This research project addresses the growing problem of job scam postings on online platforms. We developed an AI system that automatically analyzes job descriptions to detect fraudulent listings and protect users from phishing attacks and identity theft.

Python Ollama LLM Fine-tuning Flask Cybersecurity Human Factors

üéØ The Problem

With the shift to online job applications, fraudulent job postings have become increasingly common. These scams:

  • Steal personal information (SSN, bank details)
  • Extract money through fake "processing fees"
  • Waste job seekers' time and resources
  • Erode trust in legitimate job platforms

Our research shows that approximately 5% of online job postings exhibit characteristics of fraud.

üîß How It Works

Our system uses fine-tuned large language models to analyze job postings for scam indicators:

Red Flags Detected:

  • Sensitive Information Requests: Asking for SSN, bank details upfront
  • Financial Requirements: Demanding payment for "training" or "equipment"
  • Contact Anomalies: Personal email addresses instead of company domains
  • Salary Promises: Unrealistically high pay for minimal work
  • Company Vagueness: Lack of verifiable company information
  • Urgency Tactics: Pressure to act immediately

Technical Approach:

We fine-tuned the Llama 3.2 3B model using Ollama on a curated dataset of 17,879 job postings from Kaggle, achieving balanced learning despite the 95:5 real-to-fake ratio.
